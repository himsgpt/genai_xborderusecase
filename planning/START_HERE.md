## 🚦 Decision Framework

### When to GO
✅ 15+ customers confirmed pain  
✅ 10+ customers said "yes I'd pay"  
✅ Technical spike proved feasibility (70%+ accuracy)  
✅ You can articulate innovation in one sentence  
✅ You're genuinely excited (not just obligated)

### When to NO-GO
❌ <10 customers confirmed pain (not painful enough)  
❌ <5 customers said "yes I'd pay" (won't pay)  
❌ Technical spike failed (<60% accuracy) (too hard)  
❌ Can't explain innovation clearly (commodity risk)  
❌ You're dreading this (burnout risk)

### When to PIVOT
🔄 Pain exists but wrong customer segment → try different segment  
🔄 Technical approach doesn't work → try simpler method  
🔄 Value prop unclear → reframe problem/solution

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Mistake 1: Skipping Validation
"I'll just build it quickly and see if people like it."

**Why this fails:** You waste 4+ weeks building something nobody wants (like your last MVP).

**Do this instead:** Interview 20 people FIRST. If <10 say "yes I'd pay", STOP.

### ❌ Mistake 2: Over-Engineering
"Let me add multi-PSP support, real-time alerts, mobile app..."

**Why this fails:** 6-month build cycle, never ship, lose motivation.

**Do this instead:** Ship 3 corridors + Stripe only. Add features AFTER first 10 customers.

### ❌ Mistake 3: Building in Isolation
"I'll launch when it's perfect. No need to show anyone yet."

**Why this fails:** No feedback loop, product misses mark, launch flops.

**Do this instead:** Beta test Week 5. Get 5 users using it. Fix issues. THEN launch.

### ❌ Mistake 4: Ignoring Metrics
"I feel like it's going well..."

**Why this fails:** Flying blind, can't diagnose problems, slow to pivot.

**Do this instead:** Track signups, conversion, churn, engagement WEEKLY. Make data-driven decisions.

### ❌ Mistake 5: Burning Out
"I'll work 100 hours/week until this succeeds."

**Why this fails:** Burnout by Week 6, quit, business dies.

**Do this instead:** 40-50 hours/week max. No work Sundays. 8-hour sleep. Sustainable pace.
